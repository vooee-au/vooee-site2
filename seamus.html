Business strategy

The Black Raven / Caravel question.

Your agency mentor is pushing for consolidation. Before you do anything, here's the honest picture.

Black Raven Media

Activations & big brands

Clients like Subway, Domino's. Pays salaries, which makes it look less profitable on paper. Strong social presence focused on activation work.

Caravel Media

Digital for smaller businesses

Recurring clients like City Fertility, BodyTrack, Diamonds International. Appears more profitable because it doesn't carry the salary overhead, but that's a financial distortion, not a real reflection.

The real risk with merging now

Black Raven's activations-focused social presence may put off the smaller digital clients who come through Caravel. The brand association doesn't naturally transfer. And the LinkedIn posting confusion (which account posts what) is a symptom of the same tension, not just an admin problem.

The one-way / two-way door framework

Before making any structural decision, ask: is this reversible? A full merger is a one-way door, very hard to undo. Offering digital services under the Black Raven name is a two-way door, you can test it and pull back if it doesn't land.

Two-way door: do this first

Start offering digital services under Black Raven as a test, without legally merging. See how clients respond. Make the brands look like sister companies with consistent visual identity.

One-way door: don't rush this

Full consolidation, dissolving Caravel. Only make this move once you have three-year revenue modelling for each scenario and know which client category is actually most profitable.
