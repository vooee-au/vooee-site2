01
Idea one of three

Kaizen.

Small improvements. Not one big transformation.

There will not be one AI tool that does everything. There will not be one agent that does everything. Add one thing that earns its place. Then another. Orchestrate them later.

02
Idea two of three

Wabi-sabi.

Nothing is ever finished.

Not just imperfection. The acceptance that things are incomplete, that they evolve. Perfection is temporary. Progress is what lasts. Don't wait for the perfect AI tool. It won't stay perfect for long.

03
Idea three of three

Kintsugi.

The gold is the glue.

Your business is the vase. The work is not to replace it. Keep what works. Add AI where it earns its place. That is the gold.

How generative AI works

A predictor.
Not a thinker.

It finishes the sentence. It does not do the maths.

Which is why it can't build your Markov models.
And why agentic AI, which uses real tools, changes that.

And the shift that changes everything

Agentic AI.

A thinker that can plan and act.

The model decides what needs doing, breaks it into steps, calls the right tool for each one, reads the result, and adjusts. A calculator when it needs to calculate. A browser when it needs to read. A database when it needs a record. The loop is the thing. That's how useful work actually gets done.

Four jobs. Four tool shapes.
